Creative Process


Salvador Dali used several techniques that helped him become creative.

His most popular being a self-induced paranoid state.
He would stimulate paranoia to create irrational associations between objects and then paint the hallucinatory images. He would hold a spoon over a tin plate until he fell asleep, then would start to draw as soon as the spoon hit the plate.
To become semi-lucid, he would stand on his head until he almost passed out.
Drinking a cocktail mixture of absinthe and eggs on a regular basis.
Absinthe having hallucinatory properties would often give him inspirations for his surrealistic paintings.
There was speculation that he indulged in LSD, however his cryptic responses to whoever asked, never determined the truth I do not take drugs I AM drug. DO NOT take LSD take Dali
