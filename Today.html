Why he is still relevant today?



Salvador Dali is known to be a famous painter, one of his most known

The persistence of memory. He became notorious for his colorful personality, and characteristic flamboyant style. For many years Dali was part of the Surrealistn movement.

His contribution to the movement was something called paranoiac-critical method, it was an exercise to access the subconscious to enhance artistic creativity. He would use this to create a reality from his dreams. This gave him the ability to mentally change reality to what he wanted, for him this became a way of life.

During his time Salvador Dali was working with styles of impressionism, futurism and cubism. His paintings became associated with 3 themes:
+ Man universe and sensations
+ Sexual symbolism
+ Ideographic imagery



His use of strange hallucinatory characters was influenced by Renaissance and contradicted the unreal dream. Dali went through a period he called the nuclear mysticism in which he created 19 large canvases. His artwork had taken on a technical brilliance that would combine detail with imagination using images, geometry and religious themes. In 1074 The Teatro- Museo opened where it featured the largest Surrealist structure, it contained a series of spaces that formed a single artistic object. His creativity and originality in his work is what made him known and is why it was a huge impact on society and the reason he is known today.
